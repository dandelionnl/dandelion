Production

Design Elements

  • COLOR PALETTE: Middle-aged singles are likely to be attracted to colors that convey warmth, comfort, and sophistication. For the message we want to send, we use colors that convey a sense of boldness, sincerity, and inspiration. When you look at our values - which are connection, confidence, and trust - we tried to find colors that complement that. Based on this we created a color palette that feels both calm and hopeful The color #E63946 (the deep red) is associated with passion, energy, and love, which may appeal to singles seeking companionship, but also the passionate feeling you get when you think about experiences. The color #F7A6A4 (the soft pink) is calming and nurturing, suggesting a sense of care and compassion, but also a sense of safety to explore. The color #FAF3EE ( the light beige) conveys a sense of simplicity, elegance, and refinement, which may appeal to those seeking a more mature and sophisticated lifestyle. Next to that, we used it for most of the buttons/background elements to create a more grounded feeling for the website. The color #457B9D (the light cool blue) stands for freedom, imagination, and inspiration, which directly connects to the core value of stepping out of your comfort zone. It also radiates a sense of security, which relates to the target audience. #1D3557 (the deep navy) suggests stability, trustworthiness, and reliability, which may resonate with singles seeking a long-term commitment. Overall, these colors convey a sense of warmth, comfort, and stability, which are qualities that middle-aged singles may value in their search for love and companionship.
  • FONT CHOICES: The font we chose is bold and mature, as well as elegant and timeless. This is directly in connection with our target audience, which is middle-aged people. We want to clearly communicate to them that we are trustworthy and serious about our brand’s intentions, while also promoting a ‘get out of your comfort zone’ mindset. We want them to be able to relate to us and expect that this font will bring a sense of familiarity to them.
  • User interface patterns (e.g. grids, carousels, menu organizations etc.) The way the navigation bar is organized was aimed to be simple and easy to navigate. We have 4 navigation links that are all equally important. Since the website’s general goal is to be a source for crowdfunding, we decided to place the ‘Support us’ navigation link in second place after the landing page. The third navigation link is the ‘Go dandelion’ page, which has the concept of our product explained, so it is aimed at bettering the understanding of potential customers of what it is that we offer. After that, we have our justifications page. In the landing (home) page, we used 2 carousels in order to make the website a bit more interactive and interesting to look at. We used two different carousels because we felt like since the two of them have two different functions they have to be differentiated in some way. The carousel used for the values of our brand is bigger and bolder, including explanations of our values, so it’s clear what we want to communicate to the customer. The second carousel is meant to introduce the team behind Dandelion and their positions. When the carousel hovers over it stops, and it can additionally also be dragged manually by the user, which makes it easy to navigate and actually looks at all the photos equally. In our ‘Go Dandelion’ page we used a column layout to spread the text equally and make the page look more appealing to the reader. We wanted to include a bit more text in a way that doesn’t make the user think that it’s too much or too boring to read, so we divided it in two to in a way ‘trick’ the customer that it’s not a long read. In our ‘Support us’ page we included a card on the bottom. That way the text on the card, including the button stands out more and brings the attention of the user immediately there. The aim was to get more donations and have more people click on the button, which leads to our GoFundme page. 
  •  The structure of the navigation and content (e.g. how content units are distributed across pages) We have decided to put most of the information about our brand on the landing (home) page so that the user can immediately see who we are, what we do and why we want to do it. It doesn’t require a lot of work to go through the information and that’s what we aimed for – easy and simple navigation of the content. Additionally, the information added has buttons underneath it to ‘find out more’. That way the reader can also interact with the website in a way and is left curious to learn more. We have the vision, values, a snippet of our product description and the crowdfunding call on our landing page – enough to keep customers intrigued and at the same time not an overwhelming amount so that it becomes boring. We decided to not create a separate ‘about us’ or ‘meet the team’ page because the personality of our team isn’t our selling point – it’s the brand. Our ‘Support us’ page is very simple and attention-grabbing, especially the part where the ‘donate’ button is. We wanted to accentuate that and make it simple to read and understand. Overcomplicating or overcrowding the content on that page specifically would probably lead to less interest from the user. The ‘Go Dandelion’ page has a similar content distribution – information on top and button on the bottom that leads to a login form. As explained before, the columns were used for the distribution of the text. The text aims to introduce the users to our concept/product more in-depth and make them engaged. The ‘Free trial’ button’s aim is to make the customer interact with the page.

Marketing

Context of campaign and promotional activities

Dandelion is a brand dedicated to middle aged singles. The dandelion symbolizes letting go and going with the flow. Trusting the process. Blowing all the seeds of a dandelion symbolizes good fate, a fighter that refuses to follow rules or regulations. We want to offer an environment of opportunity for everyone who is open to meeting someone special with the same intentions. The goal is to find someone to have by their side to do fun activities with and just experience life with someone again. Many people find it difficult to find a safe dating environment and that's exactly what we are. We want to help our visitors find their values ​​and boost their self-confidence to look for a partner in a safe environment. Our vision is therefore: “In a world full of insecurities and expectations that hold you back, we believe that you should take a leap of faith and not only trust yourself but trust the process; only then will you be open and confident enough to let go and be your true self.”

Here are some of our objectives, see appendix media communication plan for more objectives

  • We want to reach 500 Page views on Instagram in total by the 10th of April. 
  • We want to reach 100 accounts on our Facebook by the 10th of April. 
  • Make them feel free to open up with new people. 
  • We want to gain the confidence of our users 
  • We want 300 likes in total on the 10th of April. 
  • We want 15 comments on our posts in total by April 10th. 
  • Create a safe dating environment

Learning Points

We didn’t reach all the objectives and we will explain why. From the interviews we conducted with our target group, we received some great insights. These insights not only helped us define our content and product objectives but also some of the marketing objectives that helped us define our campaign. We learned that in defining marketing objectives, you have to really place yourself in the mindset of the target audience. How we did this was by looking at our target audience’s media behavior. For example how they spend the most time on social media during 7-9 am and 8-10 pm. This is then what we based our campaign schedule on, to make sure that our content would have the most effective reach to our target audience. We also learned that looking at why and how we post content has an immediate effect on whom we reach. For example, the Dandelion Instagram account is now following companies that have a direct link to the target group. This is so the algorithm will push our account to the desired potential consumers of our brand. During the defining period of our communication and media plan, we expected to reach all of our goals. Because we presumed that the objectives we set at the start were rational and reachable, when we launched our social media accounts we expected the following to start growing immediately. This didn’t really happen. Our following started growing when we took a more proactive approach to following companies and accounts with a direct link to the target audience. Something we didn’t expect to happen, was being able to have a live feed on google analytics. Because none of our team members were familiar with the program, it was a very surprising and helpful tool to look at our website’s engagement. The first thing we ran into was coming up with a name and a logo and the use of color. In terms of marketing, we mainly used Instagram and Facebook and this worked well. If we were to continue with this brand/company, we would like to set up a campaign to ensure that we achieve more brand awareness in a different way. We would do this through a physical campaign and people on the street or in a place where many people of our target group come to stand and address them. Validation of Assumptions

While looking at our problem interviews and the lean canvas version that was created after these interviews, a couple of things came to our attention. Firstly, we noticed that we didn’t have a great unfair advantage. Our concept was very basic, which was a good place to start, but also resulted in the fact that we didn’t stand out from other similar brands on the market. Second, we hadn’t established any key metrics yet. This made it hard for us to understand how our target audience would be using our services. However, these missing parts also came from the fact that we hadn’t specified a single product yet. By doing more research on the target audience, coming up with ideas on how to tackle these lacking parts of the lean canvas, and creating multiple product concepts, we began working on our demos for the solution interviews. We decided on having three demos, which were all different products that fit within the core values of our brand. After these solution interviews, we were able to define our unfair advantage and our key metrics, which resulted in our final lean canvas.
